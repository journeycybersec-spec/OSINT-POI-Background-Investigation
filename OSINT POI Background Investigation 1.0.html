0. Purpose, Scope & Engagement Rules Define why you are investigating and what is in-bounds.
‚ñº
‚ö†Ô∏è Legal & Ethical Baseline
Only use publicly accessible OSINT. No hacking, intrusion, bypassing authentication, or harassment. Confirm your jurisdiction's privacy and data protection laws.

0.1 Purpose of This Methodology

This methodology guides an investigator through a complete, repeatable OSINT workflow for background checks on individuals, using open sources only. It is suitable for due diligence, pre-employment vetting, executive checks, internal investigations, and threat/risk analysis.

0.2 Engagement Rules

  • No unauthorized access: No credential stuffing, bypassing logins, or exploiting vulnerabilities.
  • No direct contact with the person of interest (POI) unless explicitly authorized as part of a social engineering engagement.
  • Respect scope: Only collect what is relevant to the agreed purpose.
  • Preserve evidence: Use screenshots, URLs, timestamps, and archives consistently.

üí° Why This Matters
Personal and identity verification is the foundation of every OSINT investigation. Before you can correlate accounts, assess risk, or build a behavioral profile, you must confirm that the person you are investigating is who they claim to be.   • Contact Points ‚Üí Validates emails, phone numbers, and other identifiers used on social platforms or accounts.
  • Cross-Dataset Consistency ‚Üí Ensures that social media, government records, and third-party databases align.
  • Fraud or Impersonation Red Flags ‚Üí Detects mismatched photos, conflicting details, or identity manipulation.
